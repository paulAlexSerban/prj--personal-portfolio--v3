## Problem

I wanted to study from my own notes without a backend, an account, or a paid SaaS — and I wanted to swap scheduling algorithms without rewriting the app.

## Approach

Questions are authored as MDX next to the posts they belong to, ingested into SQLite, and exported as a static JSON bundle. The web app is fully client-side: it loads the bundle, tracks learning state in the browser, and feeds reviews to a pluggable scheduler.

- **Scheduler as a seam** — SM-2 and FSRS implement one interface; switching is a config flag.
- **Versioned local state** so storage can evolve without losing a user's history.
- **No accounts** in v1 — everything lives in the browser.

## Outcome

A genuinely offline-capable study tool that reuses the blog's content with zero duplication, and a clean place to experiment with scheduling research.
